ASTROLOGICAL ASPECTS — Reference Guide

Aspects are angular relationships between planets in a birth chart. They describe how planetary energies interact — whether they harmonize, challenge, or intensify each other. Aspects are the conversations between planets.

MAJOR ASPECTS (Ptolemaic)

CONJUNCTION (0°, orb ±8°)
The most powerful aspect. Two planets occupy the same degree of the zodiac, merging their energies into a single force. The nature of a conjunction depends entirely on which planets are involved — Venus conjunct Jupiter is profoundly different from Mars conjunct Saturn. Conjunctions amplify, intensify, and fuse. The native may have difficulty separating the two energies.
Example: Sun conjunct Moon — emotional nature and identity are deeply intertwined. The person acts and feels as one unified force. Born near a New Moon.

OPPOSITION (180°, orb ±8°)
Two planets face each other across the zodiac, creating a polarity of tension and awareness. Oppositions demand integration — learning to honor both ends of the axis. They often manifest through projection: seeing one planet's qualities in other people rather than within oneself. Oppositions create relationship dynamics and require balance.
Example: Venus opposite Saturn — love (Venus) vs. duty and restriction (Saturn). The native may experience love as something that must be earned, or swing between warmth and withdrawal.

TRINE (120°, orb ±6°)
The most harmonious major aspect. Planets in trine share the same element (fire, earth, air, or water), creating a natural flow of energy. Trines represent innate talents and easy gifts — things that come naturally. The risk is complacency; because the energy flows without friction, the native may take these gifts for granted.
Example: Moon trine Neptune — natural psychic sensitivity, creative imagination, and emotional depth. The person may have vivid dreams and deep empathy without trying.

SQUARE (90°, orb ±6°)
The aspect of dynamic tension and growth. Squares create friction between planets that share the same modality (cardinal, fixed, mutable) but clash in element. They generate the energy needed for growth, achievement, and transformation. Squares are not "bad" — they are the engine of the chart, driving the native to overcome obstacles and develop strength.
Example: Mars square Pluto — raw power meets deep intensity. This can manifest as tremendous willpower and ambition, but also power struggles and issues with control.

SEXTILE (60°, orb ±4°)
A gentle, supportive aspect between planets in compatible elements. Sextiles represent opportunities that require some effort to activate — unlike trines, they don't happen automatically. They indicate skills that can be developed and doors that open when you knock. Sextiles are the "helping hand" aspects.
Example: Mercury sextile Uranus — mental agility and original thinking are available when cultivated. The person has flashes of insight and can connect unusual ideas.

MINOR ASPECTS

QUINCUNX / INCONJUNCT (150°, orb ±2°)
An awkward, adjusting energy between planets with nothing in common (different element and modality). Quincunxes create a sense of disconnect — the two areas of life don't naturally communicate. Requires constant adjustment and health awareness. Often linked to psychosomatic patterns.

SEMI-SEXTILE (30°, orb ±2°)
Adjacent signs have a subtle, slightly irritating connection. Growth through small adjustments and awareness of neighboring life areas.

QUINTILE (72°, orb ±2°)
A creative, talented aspect linked to special abilities and unique gifts. Quintiles indicate areas where the native possesses an almost magical creative capacity.

ASPECT PATTERNS

GRAND TRINE: Three planets each 120° apart, forming an equilateral triangle. Enormous talent in the element involved, but risk of laziness or taking gifts for granted. Requires a "release point" (planet aspecting the trine) to activate its potential.

T-SQUARE: Two planets in opposition with a third planet squaring both. Creates intense drive and a focal point of energy at the apex planet. The "empty leg" of the grand cross shows where balance is needed.

GRAND CROSS: Four planets at 90° intervals forming a cross. Tremendous tension but also tremendous drive. The native faces challenges from all sides but develops remarkable resilience and capability.

YOD (Finger of God): Two planets in sextile, both quincunx to a third "apex" planet. Creates a sense of fated mission or special purpose. The apex planet carries a destined quality that demands expression.

STELLIUM: Three or more planets in the same sign or house. Concentrates energy powerfully in one area of life. The sign and house of the stellium become the dominant theme of the chart.

ORBS AND APPLICATION
An aspect is "applying" when the faster planet is approaching the exact angle — applying aspects are stronger and represent future events or developing dynamics. A "separating" aspect is past exact — its energy is waning. Tighter orbs indicate stronger aspects.

INTERPRETING ASPECTS
Always consider:
1. Which planets are involved and their essential nature
2. The type of aspect (harmonious vs. challenging)
3. Whether the aspect is applying or separating
4. The houses ruled by the aspecting planets
5. The signs involved (even in the same aspect type, different sign combinations shift the expression)
6. Whether the aspect is part of a larger pattern (grand trine, T-square, etc.)
