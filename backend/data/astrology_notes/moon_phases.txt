MOON PHASES — Astrological Reference

The Moon's phases reflect the cyclic relationship between the Sun and Moon. Each phase carries a distinct energy that colors the emotional and instinctive nature of anyone born during it, and influences the timing of events.

THE EIGHT LUNAR PHASES

NEW MOON (0°–44° ahead of Sun)
The New Moon is a time of beginnings, fresh starts, and planting seeds. The sky is dark, and the Moon is invisible — this is the phase of potential, not yet manifest. Those born under a New Moon are initiators, driven by instinct rather than reflection. They act on impulse and are comfortable starting things without knowing the full picture. New Moon energy favors setting intentions, beginning projects, and turning inward for clarity.

CRESCENT MOON (45°–89° ahead of Sun)
The Crescent phase is about emerging and pushing forward against resistance. The first sliver of light appears. Those born under a Crescent Moon are fighters for their visions — they must overcome early obstacles and self-doubt. This phase favors taking action on intentions set at the New Moon, gathering resources, and building momentum despite challenges.

FIRST QUARTER MOON (90°–134° ahead of Sun)
The First Quarter represents crisis in action — a turning point where commitment is tested. Half the Moon is illuminated, symbolizing a choice between continuing forward or falling back. Those born at this phase are dynamic, decisive, and action-oriented. They thrive under pressure. This phase favors making decisions, taking bold action, and resolving conflicts.

GIBBOUS MOON (135°–179° ahead of Sun)
The Gibbous phase is about refinement, adjustment, and preparation for fullness. More than half the Moon is lit, and there's a sense of "almost there." Those born under a Gibbous Moon are perfectionists and analysts, constantly seeking to improve. This phase favors editing, refining, and making final adjustments before completion.

FULL MOON (180°–224° ahead of Sun)
The Full Moon is the climax of the lunar cycle — maximum illumination, awareness, and revelation. What was hidden at the New Moon is now fully visible. Those born under a Full Moon live in the tension of polarity (Sun and Moon in opposition), seeking to integrate opposing forces within themselves. Full Moon energy favors culmination, harvesting results, celebrating achievements, and gaining clarity through relationship mirrors.

DISSEMINATING MOON (225°–269° ahead of Sun)
The Disseminating phase is about sharing, teaching, and distributing what was learned at the Full Moon. The light begins to wane. Those born in this phase are natural teachers, communicators, and evangelists for ideas. This phase favors sharing knowledge, mentoring, and spreading your message.

LAST QUARTER MOON (270°–314° ahead of Sun)
The Last Quarter represents crisis in consciousness — a reorientation of values and beliefs. Half the Moon is again illuminated, but now it's the left half (waning). Those born at this phase are revisionary, often challenging the status quo and dismantling what no longer serves. This phase favors releasing old patterns, forgiving, and making space for what comes next.

BALSAMIC MOON (315°–359° ahead of Sun)
The Balsamic Moon is the final phase — a time of closure, surrender, and gestation. Only a thin crescent remains before the cycle renews. Those born under a Balsamic Moon are old souls with a sense of destiny or karmic completion. They may feel called to sacrifice, serve, or prepare the ground for future generations. This phase favors meditation, letting go, rest, and spiritual practice.

MOON PHASES IN TRANSIT
When the transiting Moon moves through its phases each month, the same energies are available to everyone:
- New Moon: Set intentions for the month. What do you want to grow?
- Full Moon: Harvest and release. What has come to fruition? What needs to be let go?
- Quarter Moons: Decision points. What adjustments are needed?

The sign of each New and Full Moon activates different houses in your natal chart, personalizing the energy. A New Moon in your 7th House emphasizes new beginnings in partnership. A Full Moon in your 10th House brings career matters to a head.

ECLIPSES
Eclipses are supercharged New and Full Moons that occur when the lunar nodes are involved.
- Solar Eclipse (New Moon near a Node): Powerful new beginnings, fated events, doors opening. Effects can unfold over 6 months.
- Lunar Eclipse (Full Moon near a Node): Intense revelations, endings, and emotional breakthroughs. Hidden truths come to light.

Eclipses near your natal planets or angles are especially significant and mark turning points in the areas of life governed by those planets.

VOID-OF-COURSE MOON
The Moon is "void of course" when it has made its last major aspect in a sign before entering the next sign. During void-of-course periods, things initiated tend not to develop as expected. It's best to avoid launching new ventures, signing contracts, or making major decisions. Use the time for routine tasks, rest, and reflection.
